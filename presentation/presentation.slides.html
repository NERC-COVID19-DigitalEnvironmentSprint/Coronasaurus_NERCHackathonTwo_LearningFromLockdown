What are the targets? ⮟¶

Paris Agreeement ⮟¶

The signatories of the Paris Agreement agree to:

  • Keep global temperature rise to well below 2°C [1].

In order to meet this the EU and its member states agree a Nationally Determined Contribution to:

  • Reduce the EU's CO2e emissions to 40% below 1990 levels by 2030 [2].

Climate Change Act ⮟¶

The UK has set it's own targets in the form of "budgets" that it can emit in a given period [3]. These are:

Period Budget (MtCO2e)
2008-2012 3,018
2013-2017 2,728
2018-2022 2,632*
2023-2027 1,950
2028-2032 1,725

Which, in the form of targets, are approximately:

Year Target (below 1990 levels)
2020 37%
2025 51%
2030 57%

* This includes 88 MtCO2e carried over as part of the surplus from the previous period's budget [4].

Amendment¶

In June 2019 the act was amended to add a net zero target by 2020 [5].

What about Brexit? ⮞¶

The Brexit transition period ends on 1st January 2021, at which point it'll need to decide its own NDCs. Current suggestions are that this could align with the existing goals of 57% but should aim be more ambitious [1].
